Matcha & Ube Run

soft cafe finds, pretty drinks,
and reels people save for later

camera first.
sip second.

This is the format for cafe and drink-led collaborations: a warm opening shot, texture, taste notes, a quick outfit or routine tie-in, and a save-worthy recommendation.

What makes it work:

  • 01 strong hook in the first two seconds
  • 02 drink texture: whisk, pour, foam, first sip
  • 03 outfit or post-workout context
  • 04 honest taste note and price/value cue
  • 05 save/share CTA that feels natural

^ made for cafes, wellness drinks, limited drops, and soft-launch menus.

Reel flow:

1.

Open with the vibe

A storefront, a gym-to-cafe walk, or a close-up of the drink landing on the table. The first frame should tell people why to keep watching.

soft, bright, not overproduced.

2.

Show the texture

Whisked matcha, ube swirl, foam, straw tap, cup sleeve, first sip. These are the details that make a recommendation feel sensory.

texture sells the craving.

3.

Add the human bit

A quick taste reaction, a friend clip, an outfit mirror shot, or a post-workout moment keeps the reel from feeling like an ad.

people follow people, not cups.

4.

Make it useful

Mention what to order, when to go, who would like it, and whether it is worth the hype. Useful details make the reel saveable.

pretty plus practical wins.

5.

Close with a save cue

End with a clear recommendation, brand tag, and a reason to save: weekend cafe list, post-gym drink, or ube dessert run.

save this for your next matcha plan.

Matcha and ube content is not just a drink shot. It is a mood, a plan, and a reason to send the reel to a friend.
